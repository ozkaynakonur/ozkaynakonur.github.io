I am a doctoral candidate in Applied Linguistics (Multilingual Language Education) and a Graduate Teaching Associate at the Intercultural English Language Programs (IELP) at The Ohio State University. I hold an MA in Teaching English as a Foreign Language (TEFL) from Bilkent University and a BA in English Language Teaching (ELT) from Gazi University. My research examines how language ideologies, particularly accentism and native-speakerism, shape judgments of legitimacy and competence in English language education. Drawing on raciolinguistics, educational sociolinguistics, and critical discourse analysis, my work focuses on teacher education contexts in Türkiye and the United States.
