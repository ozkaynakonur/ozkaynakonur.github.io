Onur Özkaynak

PhD Candidate in Multilingual Language Education
The Ohio State University

I am a PhD candidate in Multilingual Language Education at The Ohio State University and a Graduate Teaching Associate in the Intercultural English Language Programs (IELP). I hold an MA in Teaching English as a Foreign Language (TEFL) and a BA in English Language Teaching (ELT).

My research examines how language ideologies—particularly accentism and native-speakerism—shape judgments of legitimacy, competence, and professionalism in English language education. Drawing on educational sociolinguistics, raciolinguistics, and critical discourse analysis, my work focuses on TESOL teacher education and multilingual higher education contexts.

Methodologically, I work with qualitative and mixed-methods designs, including interviews, critical discourse analysis, and experimental approaches such as verbal guise tasks.

Teaching

My teaching focuses on language teacher education and multilingual writing. Across undergraduate, graduate, and adult education contexts, I design instruction that foregrounds linguistic diversity, critical language awareness, and equitable access to academic participation.

Pedagogically, I draw on principles from educational linguistics and translingual approaches to support multilingual learners' rhetorical flexibility, disciplinary writing practices, and critical engagement with language norms.

Research & Methodological Expertise

My research adopts mixed-methods designs grounded in critical sociolinguistics. I integrate quantitative and qualitative approaches to examine language ideologies, accentism, multilingual education, and teacher identity construction across higher education and teacher education contexts.

Theoretically, my work is informed by raciolinguistics, educational sociolinguistics, Global Englishes, and critical discourse analysis, with a focus on how institutional norms, market logics, and racialized language ideologies shape evaluations of linguistic legitimacy and professional competence.

Quantitative Research

  • Experimental, survey-based, and explanatory sequential designs
  • Descriptive and inferential statistics (t-tests, ANOVA, MANOVA, regression)
  • Questionnaire design, scale development, and validation
  • Exploratory and confirmatory factor analysis (EFA/CFA)
  • Structural Equation Modeling (PLS-SEM)
  • Statistical tools: SPSS, Jamovi, SmartPLS

Qualitative Research

  • Semi-structured and phenomenological interviews
  • Qualitative surveys and participant observation
  • Reflexive thematic analysis and content analysis
  • Critical discourse analysis
  • Pattern coding and analytic memo writing
  • Software: Dedoose, Avidnote, Qualtrics

Mixed Methods Research

  • Explanatory sequential mixed-methods designs (quant → qual)
  • Integration of statistical trends with qualitative themes
  • Focus on linking numerical patterns with critical narrative accounts

Research Tools & Technologies

  • Praat (acoustic and phonetic analysis)
  • Qualtrics (survey design and administration)
  • Dedoose (qualitative and mixed-methods analysis)
  • SPSS, Jamovi, SmartPLS (quantitative analysis)
