Onur Özkaynak

PhD Candidate in Multilingual Language Education
The Ohio State University

I am a PhD candidate in Multilingual Language Education at The Ohio State University and a Graduate Teaching Associate in the Intercultural English Language Programs (IELP). I hold an MA in Teaching English as a Foreign Language (TEFL) from Bilkent University and a BA in English Language Teaching (ELT) from Gazi University. Prior to joining OSU, I taught academic English in higher education contexts in Türkiye and Finland for several years.

My research examines how language ideologies—particularly accentism and native-speakerism—shape judgments of legitimacy, competence, and professionalism in English language education. Drawing on educational sociolinguistics, raciolinguistics, and critical discourse analysis, my work focuses on TESOL teacher education and multilingual higher education contexts in Türkiye and the United States.

Methodologically, I work with qualitative and mixed-methods designs, including interviews, critical discourse analysis, and experimental approaches such as verbal guise tasks. Across my research and teaching, I am interested in how institutional norms, market logics, and racialized language ideologies influence whose English is valued, whose voices are legitimized, and how multilingual speakers navigate these structures.
